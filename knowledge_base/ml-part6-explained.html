Part 1: Generative AI Fundamentals
Latent space, embeddings, and probability distributions
▼

Three core concepts underpin all generative models:

ConceptWhat It IsExample
Latent SpaceA compressed, lower-dimensional representation that captures the essential features of the dataA 512×512 image → 64×64 latent (48× compression)
EmbeddingsDense vector representations where similar concepts are close together in space"cat" and "dog" vectors are close; "cat" and "airplane" are far apart
Probability DistributionsGenerative models learn the distribution of data, then sample from it to create new examplesSampling from the "face distribution" creates new, realistic faces
🏫 School Analogy: Latent space is like a map of a city — it's much smaller than the actual city but still captures where everything is relative to everything else. Embeddings are like placing every word in a classroom — synonyms sit near each other, antonyms sit far apart. Sampling is like randomly pointing at the map and building whatever's at that location.
💡 Key Insight: Similar concepts cluster together in latent space, and you can smoothly interpolate between them. Moving gradually between the latent code for "cat" and "dog" produces images that look like cat-dog hybrids — this wouldn't be possible in pixel space!

The Core Idea: Destroy, Then Reconstruct

Diffusion models work by learning to reverse the process of adding noise. During training, you gradually destroy an image by adding noise step-by-step. The model learns to reverse each step — so at generation time, you start with pure noise and denoise it into a coherent image.

The 3 Components of Stable Diffusion

ComponentRoleDetails
1. CLIP Text EncoderUnderstands your text prompt"A cat on a beach" → 77 tokens × 768 dimensions
2. U-Net DenoiserThe brain — removes noise step by step, guided by the text860M–2.6B parameters. Uses cross-attention to link text ↔ image
3. VAE (Decoder)Expands the latent result back to a full image64×64×4 latent → 512×512×3 pixel image
🏫 School Analogy: Imagine you're restoring a damaged painting. The CLIP encoder is like reading the description of what the painting should look like. The U-Net is the restorer who carefully removes damage layer by layer, constantly checking the description. The VAE decoder is like putting the final coat of varnish and framing it at full size.

Key Parameters You Can Tune

ParameterWhat It ControlsSweet Spot
CFG ScaleHow closely the image follows your prompt. Low = creative, high = precise but may have artifacts7–10
StepsHow many denoising iterations. More = better quality but slower20–30
SamplerThe algorithm for denoising (PNDM, DDIM, DPM)DPM++ 2M Karras
📌 Why "Latent" Diffusion? Stable Diffusion works in a compressed 64×64 latent space, NOT the full 512×512 pixel space. This is 48× more efficient — which is why it can run on consumer GPUs instead of requiring a data center!

The Transformer — The Architecture Behind Everything

Published in 2017 ("Attention Is All You Need"), the Transformer replaced RNNs with self-attention — enabling parallel processing and capturing long-range dependencies. It powers ChatGPT, BERT, Stable Diffusion's text encoder, and modern TTS systems.

Transformer TypeArchitectureBest ForExamples
Encoder-onlyBidirectional — sees all tokensUnderstanding tasks (classification, NER)BERT, RoBERTa
Decoder-onlyCausal — sees only past tokensGeneration tasks (text, code)GPT, LLaMA, Claude
Encoder-DecoderBothSeq-to-seq (translation, summarization)T5, BART

Self-Attention: Query, Key, Value

The core mechanism that makes Transformers work. For each word, the model computes:

📐 The Attention Formula:
Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(Q × KT / √dk) × V

Query (Q): "What am I looking for?"
Key (K): "What do I contain?"
Value (V): "What do I output?"

Multiply Q×K to get similarity scores → softmax to get attention weights → multiply by V to get the weighted output.
🏫 School Analogy: Imagine you're in a library (the sentence). You have a question (Query). Each book has a title on the spine (Key). You compare your question to each title to decide which books are relevant (attention weights). Then you read the content (Value) of the relevant books. Multi-head attention is like sending 8 students to the library, each looking for different types of information!

LLMs use BPE (Byte-Pair Encoding) or SentencePiece to split text into subword tokens (~50,000 vocabulary). This handles unseen words by breaking them into known pieces.

Autoregressive Generation: One Token at a Time

Input:
"The cat"
→ predict →
Next token:
"sat" (82%)
→ append →
Input:
"The cat sat"
→ predict →
Next token:
"on" (91%)
→ ...

Each token is predicted one at a time, appended to the input, and fed back in. This continues until an end-of-sequence token or the max length is reached. Causal masking ensures the model can only see past tokens, never future ones.

Sampling Strategies — Controlling Creativity

StrategyHow It WorksEffect
GreedyAlways pick the highest-probability tokenDeterministic, repetitive, boring
TemperatureT<1 sharpens probs (focused), T>1 flattens them (random)T=0.7 is common for balanced output
Top-kOnly consider the top k most likely tokens (e.g., k=50)Filters out unlikely garbage tokens
Top-p (Nucleus)Consider tokens until cumulative probability ≥ p (e.g., p=0.9)Dynamic vocabulary — best quality in most cases
Repetition PenaltyReduce probability of already-used tokens (1.1–1.2×)Prevents "the the the the..."
🏫 School Analogy: Temperature is like an exam difficulty dial. T=0.1 means you MUST give the textbook answer (safe but boring). T=1.5 means you're encouraged to be creative and take risks (interesting but sometimes wrong). Top-p is like saying "only consider answers that are at least somewhat reasonable" — ignoring the truly wild options.

Part 5: Image Super-Resolution
Real-ESRGAN, RRDB architecture, PSNR/SSIM metrics
▼

Traditional (Bicubic)

Simple interpolation. Blurry, no new detail added. Fast but low quality.

Neural Super-Resolution

Learns to hallucinate realistic detail. Sharp textures, plausible features. Slower but stunning results.

Real-ESRGAN Architecture (RRDB)

The Residual in Residual Dense Block architecture powers Real-ESRGAN:

ComponentWhat It Does
Residual Dense BlocksMultiple conv layers with dense connections — every layer receives input from ALL previous layers for maximum feature reuse
Residual ConnectionsLocal (within each block) + Global (across network) — enables 23+ layers deep
PixelShuffle UpsamplingLearnable upsampling: 2× → 4× via two PixelShuffle layers (sub-pixel convolution)
GAN TrainingPerceptual loss produces more realistic textures vs pixel-level loss alone

Quality Metrics

MetricWhat It MeasuresGood Score
PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio)Pixel-level accuracy (dB)28–32 dB very good, 32+ excellent
SSIM (Structural Similarity)Perceptual quality (correlates better with human judgment)0.8+ good, 0.9+ excellent
🏫 School Analogy: Traditional upscaling is like zooming in on a photo — everything gets blurry. Neural super-resolution is like an artist who looks at the blurry image and paints in what the details SHOULD look like, based on having studied millions of high-res photos. It's "hallucinating" detail, but the hallucinations are educated guesses.

Summary — What You Learned!
▼
🎯 Takeaway #1 — Generative ≠ Discriminative. Parts 1–5 learned P(Y|X) — predict labels from data. Part 6 learns P(X) — model the data distribution itself and sample from it to create new content.
🎯 Takeaway #2 — Diffusion = Destroy + Reconstruct. Stable Diffusion learns to reverse noise addition. The CLIP encoder understands your text, the U-Net denoises guided by it, and the VAE decoder reconstructs the full image. Latent space makes it 48× more efficient.
🎯 Takeaway #3 — Transformers Are Everything. Self-attention (Q, K, V) enables parallel processing and long-range dependencies. LLMs use decoder-only transformers, generating text one token at a time autoregressively.
🎯 Takeaway #4 — Sampling Controls Creativity. Temperature, Top-k, and Top-p let you tune the randomness-quality tradeoff. Low temp = safe and focused. High temp = creative but risky. Top-p = best default.
🎯 Takeaway #5 — AI Creates Across Modalities. Text→Image (Stable Diffusion), Text→Text (LLMs), Text→Audio (Bark/MMS-TTS), LowRes→HiRes (Real-ESRGAN). All built on attention, latent spaces, and large-scale training.
